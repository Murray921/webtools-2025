Welcome to my Villian Story

Chapter 1 The Awakening

Cj’s awakening was not a moment of clarity, but of rage. He felt the cold stone beneath his body, the heavy weight of his armor, and the weight of betrayal. The cursed land stretched before him like a poisoned garden, but it no longer mattered. He wasn’t here to save anyone, least of all himself. Memories flooded his mind, ones he wished he could erase—betrayal from those he once called brothers. They had cast him aside, labeled him a monster, a sinner. But they were wrong. He was not the villain in this story. No, they were. They had bound him in chains, erased his name, and condemned him to a hellish existence. It was their sin, their hubris, that had led to his fall. And now, the time had come to make them pay.

Chapter 2: The Unholy Statue

As Cj roamed the shattered landscape, he found a statue—a monument to the so-called gods who had abandoned him. The statue stood tall, weeping gold tears, as if mocking the very concept of mercy. But Cj had no use for mercy. Only vengeance. He approached the statue, his sword glinting in the dim light. The moment his hand touched the stone, his mind was assaulted with the memories of the past. He saw himself—once a champion of the divine—now cast into this forsaken realm as punishment. The gods had played their games with him, and now he would play his. The statue’s tears began to bleed, and its golden eyes seemed to pierce into his soul. But Cj wasn’t afraid. He had already seen the truth—the gods were just as flawed, just as sinful as the men who had turned against him. A voice echoed in his mind, soft and mocking. "You seek redemption, but redemption is for the weak. You seek power, for only through power can you make them suffer." Cj’s grip on his sword tightened. "Then I will make them all suffer."

Chapter 3: The Pilgrimage of Wrath

The world became a twisted playground for Cj’s fury. The cursed land, full of suffering and decay, seemed to welcome him as one of its own. Every step was a step toward destruction, every fallen enemy another testament to his power. He no longer cared about the broken, the damned, or the lost. They were just tools to fuel his path of vengeance. Along the way, he encountered twisted forms of the innocent—people who had once prayed to gods who no longer listened. They whispered of salvation, of redemption, but Cj knew better. Salvation was a lie. It was a path paved with weakness and regret. Every city, every temple he passed was a reminder of the gods’ cruelty. They had allowed his fall, allowed his torment. And now, they would pay for it.

Chapter 4: The Trial of the Damned

Cj’s journey led him to the Basilica of Mercy, a place of false hope. The cathedral was adorned with symbols of salvation, but Cj knew the truth. It was a place where the faithful sought atonement, a place where they could beg for forgiveness. It sickened him. Inside, he found the priestess—a figure draped in golden robes, her face obscured by a veil. She was a symbol of all that was wrong with this world: a puppet of the divine, a willing servant to the false gods. "You seek the Redeemed," she whispered, her voice like the ringing of bells in the silence of the cathedral. "But to reach him, you must first confront your own sins." Cj’s laughter echoed through the chamber. "My sins? My only sin was trusting in false gods who abandoned me. I am not here to atone. I am here to destroy." A terrible figure emerged from the shadows—a monstrous knight, towering and armored. Cj could feel the weight of its presence, but it meant nothing to him. This was just another trial, another obstacle in his way. The battle was brutal, but Cj was relentless. With each strike, he felt his rage grow, and with each fallen enemy, his power surged. He would not be stopped.

Chapter 5: The False Redeemed

At the heart of the Basilica, Cj finally found the figure he had sought—the Redeemed. But it was not a savior standing before him. It was a twisted version of himself, a mockery of everything he had once believed in. "You are me," the Redeemed spoke, his voice cold and unforgiving. "But you have fallen too far. There is no coming back." Cj’s eyes burned with hatred. "You are not me. You are what I should have been—weak, pathetic, a puppet of the gods. I will end you, and I will end everything you stand for." The Redeemed stepped forward, his eyes filled with sorrow. "No. The true path to power is through acceptance, through embracing the pain. You will never defeat me." With a scream of fury, Cj raised his sword and plunged it into the chest of the Redeemed. But even as the figure crumpled, Cj felt no satisfaction. The gods would not be appeased. No redemption would come. There was only destruction. And he was the one who would bring it.
